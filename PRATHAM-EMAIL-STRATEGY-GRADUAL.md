## The 3-Email Sequence

### Email 1: Share Insights + Broad Questions
**Goal:** Show value, start dialogue, NO MEETING ASK
**Timing:** Send immediately (today)
**File:** PRATHAM-EMAIL-1-INSIGHTS-QUESTIONS.md

**What it does:**
- Shares analysis results (347 questions, 12 topics)
- Provides insights on content structure
- Asks 3 broad open-ended questions:
  1. Primary challenge for students?
  2. How do they access content today?
  3. Ideal experience in 6-12 months?
- Patient tone: "No rush on these"
- NO meeting suggestion

**Expected outcome:**
- They respond with context about their situation
- You learn about pain points, infrastructure, vision
- Relationship building starts

---

### Email 2: Tailored Directions + Technical Questions
**Goal:** Show you listened, suggest relevant directions, ask technical details
**Timing:** 1-2 days after they respond to Email 1
**File:** PRATHAM-EMAIL-2-TAILORED-DIRECTIONS.md

**What it does:**
- References their specific answers from Email 1
- Suggests 1-2 directions that match their pain points
- Asks technical questions:
  - Current infrastructure?
  - Student devices?
  - Online vs offline?
  - Integration vs fresh build?
- Soft meeting suggestion: "Would a call be useful, or continue email?"

**Expected outcome:**
- They answer technical questions
- They express interest in specific direction
- They might suggest call themselves

---

### Email 3: Meeting Suggestion
**Goal:** Earn the meeting through demonstrated value
**Timing:** After they respond to Email 2 with interest
**File:** PRATHAM-EMAIL-3-MEETING-SUGGESTION.md

**What it does:**
- "Based on your answers, Direction X seems best because..."
- Proposes 20-minute call with clear agenda
- Multiple time slot options
- Exit option: "Or continue via email"
- No pressure tone

**Expected outcome:**
- They schedule the call
- Or ask for more info (continue email dialogue)

## Key Principles

### 1. Patience Over Pressure
- Don't rush to the meeting ask
- Let them guide the pace
- Each email adds value independently
- If they ghost, graceful exit

### 2. Listen Before Pitching
- Email 1: Ask questions
- Email 2: Show you heard them
- Email 3: Propose relevant solution
- Never generic, always tailored

### 3. Multiple Exit Options
- They can say "not now" at any stage
- They can choose email over call
- They can disengage without awkwardness
- Professional relationship preserved

### 4. Value First, Always
- Every email shares insights or information
- Never just "checking in"
- Never "just following up"
- Always adding to the conversation

### 5. No Assumptions
- Don't assume they have an LMS
- Don't assume they have budget
- Don't assume they're tech-savvy
- Ask, don't assume

## On the Call (When You Get There)

### Call Structure (20 minutes):

**Minutes 1-5: Listen**
- "Tell me about your current setup"
- "What's working? What's not?"
- "What would make the biggest difference?"

**Minutes 6-15: Show Relevant Demo**
- "Based on what you said, let me show you..."
- Demo ONLY features that solve their pain
- Pause for questions

**Minutes 16-20: Next Steps**
- "Does this address your [pain point]?"
- "What would you need to see to move forward?"
- "Would a pilot make sense?"
- Let them guide

### After the Call:

**Same Day:**
Send summary email:
- Thank them
- Recap their pain points (show you listened)
- Recap what you showed
- Confirm next steps
- No pressure
